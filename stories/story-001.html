The day I understood my mother's silence

Shared by Mariam

I used to think my mother was quiet because she had nothing to say. Later, I understood she was carrying more than anyone knew.

She carried bills, worry, family problems, tired mornings, and the emotional weight of everyone around her. Still, when we entered the room, she smiled.

Her silence was not emptiness. It was strength. It was patience. It was a woman choosing peace even when life was not peaceful to her.

Today, I wish I could go back and thank her for every moment I failed to notice.
