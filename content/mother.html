The Mother You Are

You will probably read these words and think they belong to someone else.

Because that is what mothers do.

You spend so much of your life looking at everyone around you that you rarely stop to look at yourself.

You celebrate birthdays that are not yours. You stay awake worrying about people who are sleeping peacefully. You remember things nobody asked you to remember.

You carry responsibilities nobody notices you are carrying. And somehow, despite all of that, you keep going.

The truth is that many of the people you love will never fully understand what you gave up for them.

They will remember the meals, but not the nights you spent exhausted. They will remember arriving safely, but not the anxiety you carried until they got home.

They will remember the warmth of your presence, but not the loneliness you sometimes felt while holding everyone else together.

A mother's love is one of the few things in this world that asks for nothing in return. It gives before being asked. It forgives before hearing an apology. It worries before danger arrives.

There are people walking this earth today with confidence because you believed in them when they doubted themselves.

You may not see your impact. But it is everywhere.

Today, for a moment, let this be about you.

Not about what you do. Not about what you provide. Not about what you sacrifice.

You.

The woman. The mother. The heart behind countless memories.

And if nobody has told you recently: thank you.

For all the things people remember, and for all the things they never knew you did.
